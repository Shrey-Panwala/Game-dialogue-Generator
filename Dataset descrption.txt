First Understand Our End Goal

We are NOT building:

❌ Customer Support Bot
❌ General Chatbot
❌ Q&A System

We ARE building:

✅ Text-Based Game Dialogue Generator

Input:

Scene: Haunted Castle

Character:
Name: Arthur
Role: Royal Guard
Personality: Loyal, Suspicious

Conversation History:
Player: Can I enter the castle?
Arthur: The king has forbidden visitors.

Player: Why?

Generate Response:

Output:

Because strange things have been happening inside these walls.

Therefore our model must learn:

How humans talk
How characters maintain personality
How emotions affect dialogue
How game NPCs behave

No single dataset contains all four.

That is why we need multiple datasets.

Dataset 1: DailyDialog
What it teaches

DailyDialog teaches:

natural conversations
multi-turn dialogue flow
proper response structure
coherent conversations

Example:

A: How was your day?
B: Pretty good. I finished my project.
A: That's great.
What we learn from it

The model learns:

Conversation Continuity

Bad:

Player: Hello

NPC: The dragon lives in the mountains.

Player: How are you?

NPC: My sword is broken.

Good:

Player: Hello

NPC: Greetings traveler.

Player: How are you?

NPC: I am doing well.

DailyDialog teaches this.

How we use it

We fine-tune the model first on DailyDialog.

Purpose:

Teach the model HOW to converse.

Think of it as primary school education.

Dataset 2: PersonaChat

This is where things become interesting.

What PersonaChat teaches

Every speaker has a persona.

Example:

Persona:
- I am a wizard.
- I love magic.
- I dislike knights.

Dialogue:
Player: What do you do?

NPC: I study ancient magic.
What we learn

Character consistency.

Without PersonaChat:

Turn 1:
I am a wizard.

Turn 5:
I am a blacksmith.

Personality drift.

Very common problem.

How we use it

Our NPCs will have:

Character:
Name: Merlin
Role: Wizard
Traits:
- Wise
- Mysterious
- Calm

During training:

Persona +
Conversation
→ Response

Example:

Persona:
Wise Wizard

Player:
Can you help me?

Response:
The answer lies in patience.

The model learns to stay in character.

What this gives us

Now our NPCs feel real.

Different NPCs speak differently.

Dataset 3: EmpatheticDialogues

This is the secret sauce.

Most dialogue models sound robotic.

What it teaches

Emotions.

Examples:

Situation:
I lost my dog.

Response:
I'm sorry to hear that.

or

Situation:
I won the tournament.

Response:
Congratulations!
What we learn

Emotion-aware responses.

Without it:

Player:
My friend died.

NPC:
Interesting.

Terrible.

With EmpatheticDialogues
Player:
My friend died.

NPC:
I am sorry for your loss.

Much better.

How we use it

Add emotional tags.

Example:

Emotion: Sad

Player:
I failed my quest.

Response:
Do not lose hope.

The model learns emotional behavior.

What this gives us

NPCs become believable.

They react emotionally.

Players feel engaged.

Dataset 4: LIGHT

This is the most important dataset.

Honestly, this dataset is almost made for your project.

What LIGHT contains

Fantasy world.

Characters.

Locations.

Objects.

Actions.

Roleplay dialogue.

Example:

Location:
Dark Forest

Character:
Elf Ranger

Object:
Magic Bow

Dialogue:
Player:
Who are you?

NPC:
I guard these woods.
What it teaches

Game-world dialogue.

What we learn

NPC behavior.

Quest interactions.

Fantasy conversations.

Roleplay.

World awareness.

Example

Without LIGHT:

Player:
Where is the dragon?

NPC:
I don't know.

Generic.

With LIGHT:

Player:
Where is the dragon?

NPC:
The beast dwells beyond the northern mountains.

Feels like a game.

How we use it

This becomes our final domain adaptation dataset.

Purpose:

Teach the model HOW GAME CHARACTERS TALK.
Training Strategy

Now comes the most important part.

DO NOT merge all datasets and train together.

That is what beginners do.

Stage 1

Train on DailyDialog

Goal:

Learn conversation structure.

Model learns:

turn taking
dialogue flow
coherence
Stage 2

Continue training on PersonaChat

Goal:

Learn character personality.

Model learns:

persona conditioning
consistency
Stage 3

Continue training on EmpatheticDialogues

Goal:

Learn emotions.

Model learns:

emotional responses
empathy
Stage 4

Continue training on LIGHT

Goal:

Learn fantasy game dialogue.

Model learns:

NPC behavior
quests
roleplay
world interaction
Final Knowledge Stack

After all stages:

        ↓
Conversation Skills

PersonaChat

Character Personality

        ↓

        ↓

Game Dialogue Generator

This is exactly the behavior we want.

For a research-grade project, I would actually go one step further and create a unified training format where all four datasets are converted into a common schema:

{
  "scene": "...",
  "persona": "...",
  "emotion": "...",
  "history": "...",
  "response": "..."
}

Then fine-tune a T5-family model (FLAN-T5) on that unified format. This will make the model much easier to control at inference time and will look far more sophisticated in your report than a simple next-utterance chatbot.
